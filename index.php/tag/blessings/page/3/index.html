October 1, 2023 GR 'Scott' Cundiff

Job 1: So do you think Job does all that out of the sheer goodness of his heart?
While the audience Satan has with the Almighty is challenging from a theological viewpoint, I think it, and this question in particular, is the absolute key to the whole book. We tend to think that the book of Job focuses in undeserved suffering and how Job responds to it, but even more basic is the issue here. The Lord points out Job‚Äôs righteousness to Satan, says that Job is his friend, and is an outstanding servant. Satan, that old accuser, replies that the only reason Job lives right and loves God is for what he gets out of it. Certainly, God has blessed Job, delighting in bringing good things into his life. Is Job a righteous man simply because it‚Äôs good business, the smart thing to do, or is he righteous because he loves the Lord and chooses to serve him? Is it so I won’t be plagued with guilt over my sin? What if all the “perks” are removed? Again, this is about as basic a question as there is.
Take Away: Why do you serve the Lord?

His nation depends on that relationship (and later on will take that relationship for granted – leading to their destruction). Still, it‚Äôs a dependable relationship that makes Israel a special nation on the face of the earth. I know that people like to use this verse to call America to God, promising that if the nation comes to the Lord that it will be blessed in all kinds of ways. No doubt, the great need of my nation is to come to God. Still, there‚Äôs a big difference between being a nation that chooses God and being a nation chosen by God.
Take Away: How blessed to be the people of the Lord!

Looking back, remaining faithful

Will David, as his vitality slips away, be put on the shelf and forgotten by not only man, but by God too? David knows that‚Äôs not going to happen. Take Away: The way to conclude life is to continue setting an example of trusting and praising the Lord.
